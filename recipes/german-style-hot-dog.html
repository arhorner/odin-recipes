German-Style Hot Dog

German-style Hot Dog with Sauerkraut and Sauteed Potatoes

This recipe takes 35 - 40 minutes to prepare and feeds 2 people.

Ingredients

  • Dijon mustard (18g)
  • Brioche style hot dog buns (2pcs)
  • Chives (5g)
  • Cider vinegar (30ml)
  • Garlic clove (1pcs)
  • Mayonnaise (50ml)
  • Pork sausage (2pcs)
  • Red onion (1pcs)
  • Shredded cabbage (150g)
  • White potato (3pcs)
  • Yellow mustard seeds (1tsp)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°c/ 200°c (Fan) and boil a kettle.
  2. Peel and finely slice your red onion.
  3. Peel and finely chop your garlic.
  4. Chop your chives finely.
  5. Heat a pot with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add you yellow mustard seeds and cook for 30 secs or until sizzling.
  6. Once sizzling, add the sliced red onion and your shredded red cabbage and cook for 4-5 min or until beginning to soften.
  7. Meanwhile, peel your potatoes and chop them into small, bite-sized pieces.
  8. Add the chopped potatoes to a separate pot with plenty of boiled water and a pinch of salt and bring to the boil over a high heat.
  9. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-high and cook for 10-12 min or until fork-tender.
  10. Once done, drain and return to the pot to steam dry.
  11. Once the onion and cabbage have softened, add half your cider vinegar (you'll use the rest later!) to the pot, cook for a further 30 secs then set aside to cool.
  12. Combine your mayonnaise and Dijon mustard in a separate bowl - this is your mustard mayo.
  13. Add your pork sausages to one side of a baking tray (use tin foil to avoid mess!) and put the tray in the oven for 15-20 min.
  14. Heat a large wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a high heat.
  15. Once hot, add the drained potatoes with a pinch of salt and cook for 5-6 min, stirring occasionally, or until golden and crisp.
  16. Once golden, add the chopped garlic and cook for a further 30 secs or until fragrant - these are your sauteed potatoes.
  17. Add the remaining cider vinegar to the cooled cabbage with a grind of black pepper and half of the chopped chives (save the rest for garnish!) and give everything a good mix up. This is your sauerkraut.
  18. Cut a slit into the top of each hot dog bun.
  19. Remove the tray with the sausages from the oven.
  20. Add the cut hot dog buns to the other side of the tray, then return the tray to the oven for 2-3 min or until warmed through.
  21. Fill each warmed hot dog bun with some mustard mayo, a sausage and some sauerkraut.
  22. Serve the sauteed potatoes to the side with the remaining sauerkraut and remaining mustard mayo.
  23. Garnish with the remaining chives and enjoy!
